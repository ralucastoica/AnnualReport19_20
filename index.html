New Initiatives

Introduction to MSC - New Online Course

We  have recently released an one-hour online course that offers new and current employees of the Meteorological Service of Canada (MSC) the opportunity to learn about their organization.

The course is made of three animated videos that introduce learners to the organizational structure of MSC, the responsibilities of its four directorates, and the products and services it provides. The videos provide an engaging learning experience and many opportunities to interact with the content through quick knowledge checks. They are narrated by MSC employees and are close captioned.

The surveys showed that employees appreciate the course for being interactive, informative, and visually appealing.

Contact Raluca Stoica or Francis Wu

Meteorologist Internship 

Following the Canadian Meteorological Centre (CMC) visit in November 2019, the TCDD piloted an internship for developmental meteorologists (MT-02s).

In order to broaden their horizons and prepare them for the future, nine senior operational MT-02s were assigned two
weeks with experienced mentors at one of the following locations: CMC development, regional labs, Canadian Ice Centre or the military services. The interns documented their experiences, by presentation or report, to share with their local prediction centre colleagues and the TCDD. Feedback has been very positive by all involved.

We will be looking at the feasibility of a longer internship for next year, but the timing may be different in light of the current
uncertainties.

Contact Mariette Kulin

Meteorologist Recruitment: Direct Entry

In addition to the annual recruitment of new trainees for the 2019-2020 initial training course, we evaluated applications from experienced meteorologists from around the world to prepare for upcoming retirements.

As we had not done so for some years, a committee was formed to develop the operational experience assessment and to interview candidates. Many thanks to Amin Erfani and Allan Rahill for their support to Michelle Hardy and Mariette Kulin of the Training Division.

A pool of qualified candidates was created and Victoria Nurse, a meteorologist from South Africa, was selected to join the Ontario Storm Prediction Centre last October. Victoria was delighted to make a "direct entry" into the MSC. We welcome her on behalf of the entire service, and feel grateful for her contribution.

Student Outreach

In order to promote our annual MT recruitment campaign, and encourage potential enrollment in meteorology and related disciplines in universities, we increased our outreach activities. We worked with the ECCC Communication branch to publish two promotional pamphlets. Several hundred pamphlets have been mailed out to academic career counsellors and the Canadian Meteorological and Oceanographic Society.

Last December, TCDD staff also presented at Dalhousie, McGill, University of Quebec at Montreal, York, University of Manitoba and University of British Columbia.

Contact Mariette Kulin, TCDD

CCMEP Training Needs   Assessment 

The CCMEP training needs assessment survey results were analyzed and shared with employees and managers. Employee representatives from the PC, CS and MT groups participated in focus groups held in early 2020.

These discussions provided invaluable input on training-related topics including priority needs and input for the upcoming PC-CS training framework. Meetings with management will be held shortly to present recommendations and discuss next steps.

Contact Ann Dacres, TCDD

Synthetic Voices

We are applying innovative eLearning techniques to train our new ice observer interns. One example is the use of synthetic voices. We have selected Wavenet synthetic voice from Google as the closest to human speech in French and English. This approach ensures a consistent high quality of audio from lesson to lesson. Furthermore, it provides flexibility when lessons are updated, thus avoiding the need to re-record the whole course audio when the narrator moves on.

A prototype lesson was created with synthetic voice and it was very well received. Subsequently, three lessons were published in the Oceanography for the Ice Observer course with this technology. The use of synthetic voice is now expanding into other Training Portal courses.

Contact Chris Sackiw, TCDD

IT Training  Committee 

We formed an IT training committee at the Canadian Meteorological Centre (CMC) last winter in response to the desire some of our new computer scientists had for more training. A CMC computer scientist (CS) chairs the group, and the members include other CSs, physical scientists and two TCDD staff.

The current members are: Jean-Philippe Gauthier, Matthew Holly, Stuart Spence, Philippe Carphin, Éric Legault-Ouellet and Stavros Antonopoulos, as well as Stephen Kerr and Nicole Bois of the TCDD.

Although the topics will be mainly related to high-performance computing and Linux systems, there will still be information and subjects that may be useful to other MSC groups. Please see the wiki website for information on our initial activities.

Contact Matthew Holly

Canadian Meteorological Centre Visit

From November 13 to 15, 2019, nine MT-02s from various forecast centres visited the Canadian Meteorological Centre (CMC) in Dorval, Quebec.  The purpose was to increase communication and collaboration within the organization by promoting exchanges between developmental meteorologists and CMC experts.

MT02s were introduced to the work done by the various Canadian Centre for Meteorological and Environmental Prediction sections and CMC experts attended presentations by MT-02s on model
performance and challenges in their forecast regions.

This visit sparked significant interest from all sides and will continue to be an annual event moving forward.

Contact: Michelle Hardy Contact: Kitty Wilkes

Communications Training for New Meteorologists

This year the MOIP 2020 students completed two days of communications training, a new initiative for the course. MOIP in Edmonton and Montreal welcomed a member of ECCC’s Media Relations team for one day of training on communicating with the media.

A second day was devoted to communicating risk and uncertainty using training material developed in-house by the TCDD. With both days being hands-on and interactive, the students enjoyed learning these topics and are better prepared for the evolving role of meteorologists at the MSC.

This material is available under the MOIP section on the Training Portal.

Contact: Kitty Wilkes, TCDD 

Revamped Numerical Weather Prediction Training Material

The NWP modules of the initial meteorologist training in MOIP had not been updated for several years. As our models have benefited from major innovations for some years now, and will become more and more important in the future for meteorologists, we developed a revamped beta version of this training in collaboration with the research and development group at CMC last fall. Developers presented to the interns in Edmonton, and researchers and developers did so in Montreal.

André Méthot has been contracted to further refine the material with our initial collaborators, along with feedback from trainees, instructors and presenters. The content will be gradually updated on the Training Portal. Be on the look out for this as it may be of interest to a wider audience. Thank you to all our partners who contributed to this major initiative.

Contact: Nicole Bois, TCDD 

Intro to Meteorology

We are revamping the online Introduction to Meteorology course, originally from Monitoring’s introductory training, to increase interactivity and learner engagement.

This course is intended for all interested employees to take part in. The first redesigned module is Pressure, a 45-minute lesson that introduces the basics of barometric pressure and pressure distribution in the atmosphere. The improvements include voice over, animations, graphics and interactive knowledge checks to assess the learners’ mastery of important concepts.

Contact: Ann Dacres and Sara Corena-Castaneda, TCDD 

Training for Ice Observer Trainers

Luc Mercier and Eric Vaillant identified a need to familiarize relatively new ice observers with on-the-job mentoring and coaching techniques used for supervising and assessing ice observer trainees in the field. Paul Ford drew from a number of sources and delivered a three-hour session on November 21, 2019 in Montreal.

The training used different strategies (e.g. online pre-quiz, presentations, videos and a role-playing exercise) and covered giving feedback, coaching and mentoring approaches. A post-session evaluation revealed that the activities were well
received and would have immediate application to their new role as mentors.

Contact: Paul Ford, TCDD
